Comprehensive monthly synthetic data for 50 areas in Harare, Zimbabwe, spanning the years 2020 through 2024.

Introduction

The Water Distribution Dataset for Harare is a meticulously crafted synthetic dataset designed to simulate water availability, demand, and priority for distribution across 50 unique geographical areas of Harare, Zimbabwe. Covering a five-year span from 2020 to 2024 on a monthly basis, this dataset enables comprehensive spatiotemporal analysis to support urban planners, researchers, and policymakers in optimizing water resource allocation and understanding the dynamics of water supply challenges in a rapidly urbanizing city.

By integrating key factors such as population density, industrial activity, and the presence of water-dependent economic activities, the dataset offers a multidimensional perspective essential to addressing water scarcity and distribution equity in the region.

Dataset Description

This dataset is intended to serve as a foundational resource for modeling and analysis of water distribution challenges in Harare. It provides synthetic but plausible data points that reflect the complex interplay between water availability and demand drivers across diverse urban landscapes.

Geographical Coverage: The dataset covers 50 distinct areas within the Harare metropolitan region. Each area is uniquely identified by an AreaID ranging from 1 to 50, representing a mix of residential neighborhoods, industrial zones, and peri-urban townships.

Temporal Coverage: Data is available monthly from January 2020 through December 2024, allowing for detailed temporal trend analysis and seasonal pattern recognition in water distribution and demand.

Data Volume: The dataset contains a total of 3,000 records, calculated as 50 areas × 5 years × 12 months, providing a robust sample size for statistical and machine learning applications.

Dataset Structure & Columns

The dataset is stored in CSV format, optimized for easy integration with data analysis tools such as Python (Pandas), R, Excel, and GIS platforms. All columns contain integer values to ensure consistency and facilitate computational efficiency.

  • industrial_activity: Proxy for industrial water consumption intensity.

Data Generation Methodology

4.1 Synthetic Data Generation Process

The dataset values were generated using a pseudo-random number generator with a fixed seed to ensure reproducibility. The ranges for each variable were carefully chosen to reflect plausible water distribution scenarios typical of an urban African city like Harare. Although the data is synthetic, it is crafted to provide realistic patterns and relationships for modeling and analysis.

4.2 Priority Assignment Logic

The distribution_priority column is computed based on combined conditions reflecting water scarcity and demand pressures:

  • High priority (2): Water availability below 30% combined with population density above 1000 people/km², or a high priority class of 4 or 5 indicating critical water-dependent economic activity.
  • Medium priority (1): Water availability below 50% with industrial activity index above 60.
  • Low priority (0): All other cases.

4.3 Assumptions and Limitations

This dataset is synthetic and should not be used as a substitute for real-world measurements. Area identifiers are anonymized and do not correspond to official administrative boundaries. Population density and industrial activity are assumed static within each month. Users should validate and calibrate models with real data when available.
